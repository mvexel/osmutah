The [OSM US Trails Stewardship Initiative](https://openstreetmap.us/our-work/trails/) pilot in southern Utah is showing real results.

## Red Cliffs Desert Reserve

The Red Cliffs Desert Reserve encompasses over 60,000 acres and is critical habitat for the [Mojave desert tortoise](https://www.fws.gov/species/desert-tortoise-gopherus-agassizii). The [Red Cliffs National Conservation Area](https://www.blm.gov/programs/national-conservation-lands/utah/red-cliffs-nca) manages the land.

## The Problem

The Prospector Trail's western end had incorrect routing data in OpenStreetMap. It directed users through **closed tortoise habitat** instead of following the official slickrock route. This wasn't just a data quality issue — it could lead people into ecologically sensitive areas.

## The Fix

I corrected the trail alignment in OpenStreetMap to follow the actual, official route along the slickrock.

## The Ripple Effect

Here's where it gets exciting. Because many outdoor apps use OpenStreetMap as their base data:

- **Trailforks** updated immediately
- **Utah Mountain Biking** reflected the change instantly
- **AllTrails** showed the correction within weeks

One edit in OpenStreetMap fixed the problem across multiple platforms.

Learn more about the initiative at [American Trails](https://www.americantrails.org/training/the-openstreetmap-us-trails-stewardship-initiative).
