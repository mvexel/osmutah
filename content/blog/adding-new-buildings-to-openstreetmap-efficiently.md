Utah is one of the fastest-growing states in the U.S., and keeping OpenStreetMap up to date with new development is an ongoing challenge.

## The Problem

Take Saratoga Springs as an example. Comparing aerial imagery from a few years ago to today shows massive development — entire new neighborhoods have sprung up. Roads and sidewalks have been mapped, but many buildings are still missing from OpenStreetMap.

## The Solution: Rapid Editor

The [Rapid Editor](https://rapideditor.org/), originally developed by Meta (formerly Facebook), makes this much easier. It integrates the **Microsoft open buildings dataset**, which shows AI-detected building outlines as purple overlays on the map.

### The Workflow

1. Open Rapid Editor and zoom to an area with missing buildings
2. Purple outlines from the Microsoft dataset appear over detected buildings
3. Click on a purple building outline
4. Press **"A"** to accept it and add it to your edit

It's that simple. Note that there's a 50-building upload limit per editing session, which helps keep edits manageable and reviewable.

This approach lets us add hundreds of buildings efficiently while still maintaining quality through the per-session limit.
